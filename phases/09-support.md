# Phase 09 — Support and iteration

## Entry

Developers/users are integrating or operating the capability and recurring questions, failures, bugs, incompatibilities, or misunderstandings are visible.

## Decisions

- Is each issue rooted in product/design, implementation, compatibility, framework/server integration, docs/sample, tooling, rollout, or messaging?
- Which patterns justify product/spec change versus guidance?
- How can fixes become durable and discoverable?

## Work and evidence

- Build an issue taxonomy with stable IDs, reproductions, environments, severity, affected segments, workaround cost, owner, and status.
- Run friction logs and inspect runtime/console/network/performance/accessibility/visual/resource evidence.
- Turn recurring questions into canonical docs, diagnostics, samples, FAQs/troubleshooting, tests, and product feedback; avoid stale duplicate knowledge.
- Track recurrence, resolution time, task success, regressions, sentiment themes, and support burden with privacy-safe denominators.
- Reopen design/standards/rollout decisions when evidence warrants it.

## Artifacts

- Public troubleshooting/known-issues material
- Reproduction and issue-routing pack
- Regression/conformance additions
- Product/spec/docs decision log
- Support health and recurrence dashboard

## Common failures

- Writing another article instead of fixing integration/product friction
- Blaming developers for ambiguous behavior
- Private support answers never reaching canonical docs
- Closing tickets without validating user task recovery

## Transition packet: support → next iteration, adoption, or deprecation

Issue patterns/denominators; validated fixes and remaining blockers; product/spec/docs changes; regression coverage; affected users; support trend; compatibility/interop impact; recommendation and next goals.
