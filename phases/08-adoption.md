# Phase 08 — Adoption and scale

## Entry

A capability is available, but useful production adoption, ecosystem integration, interoperability, or developer understanding needs to grow.

## Decisions

- What is the real adoption bottleneck by segment?
- Which multipliers remove it: product changes, frameworks, tooling, partners, GDEs, events, education, diagnostics, policy, or interoperability?
- How does adoption serve end users and the broader web rather than Chrome-only usage?

## Work and evidence

- Segment awareness, evaluation, trial, implementation, production, retention, and abandonment; do not collapse them into one adoption count.
- Find communities/customers and document jobs, integrations, blockers, decision makers, and support needs.
- Run production friction logs across frameworks, servers, deployment and user configurations.
- Build adapters/tooling/diagnostics and contribute upstream where that scales better than content.
- Create versioned DevRel-in-a-box materials for staff, GDEs, partners, meetups and educators.
- Track other-engine positions/implementation and Baseline path.

## Artifacts

- Adoption funnel with denominators/limitations
- Segment/bottleneck/community map
- Framework/tool/partner work plan
- Reusable enablement pack
- Case studies with attributable outcomes
- Interoperability/Baseline and support dashboard

## Common failures

- Broadcast plan mistaken for adoption strategy
- Registrations/downloads mistaken for production use
- Chrome-only use optimized at the expense of web compatibility
- Case studies selected without failures or denominator

## Transition packet: adoption → ongoing support/iteration

Adoption stages and segments; successful/abandoned implementations; bottlenecks and product feedback; framework/tool status; support burden; other-engine/Baseline movement; asset reuse; next product/docs/ecosystem goals; owners and monitoring.
