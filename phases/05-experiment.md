# Phase 05 — Experiment / Origin Trial

## Entry

A field experiment is justified by explicit uncertainties that require real-origin/user conditions; Intent to Experiment and Origin Trial preparation may begin.

## Decisions

- What cannot be learned behind a flag or in a developer trial?
- Which qualitative and quantitative evidence will answer it?
- Are participants, support, privacy, fallback, and exit paths adequate?
- Continue, change, extend, pause, or stop?

## Work and evidence

- State hypotheses, metrics, denominators, decision thresholds, privacy handling, and limitations before recruitment.
- Recruit representative origins/segments and record actual activation/use, not token registration alone.
- Validate token/header/server setup, feature detection, unsupported/expiry behavior, mobile/desktop, and third-party embedding where relevant.
- Provide production-quality samples, fallback, support, issue routing, incident plan, and expectations that APIs/tokens may change or end.
- Collect partner/user feedback, task outcomes, compatibility, performance/resource, support, and abuse evidence.
- Publish periodic findings, changes, sample sizes, missing data, contradictions, and extension rationale.

## Artifacts

- Experiment protocol and analysis plan
- Participant/partner ledger and representativeness
- Trial onboarding/support/FAQ
- Live issue/friction/risk dashboard
- Interim/final readout and decision record

## Common failures

- Running an OT because it is customary, not because field evidence is needed
- Registrations reported as use/adoption
- Metrics selected after results are known
- Expiry/fallback/user cost ignored
- Extension requested without answering original goals

## Transition packet: experiment → ship, rework, repeat, or stop

Original hypotheses; participant denominator and exposure; measured/qualitative results; methodology/limitations; partner evidence stage; design/docs/implementation changes; user and incident findings; unresolved interop/review risks; ship/rework/extension/stop recommendation; and conditions that would change it.
