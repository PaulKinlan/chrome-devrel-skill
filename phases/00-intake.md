# Phase 00 — Intake and classification

## Entry

Any rough idea, API proposal, launch request, artifact request, support issue, adoption problem, deprecation, initiative, or event.

## Decisions

- What mode and lifecycle stage is this actually in?
- What developer/end-user problem or organizational decision is being addressed?
- Is browser/platform intervention plausibly the right layer?
- What evidence boundary and urgency apply?
- Who owns the next decision?

## Work

- Collect available ChromeStatus, explainer/spec, intent, implementation, demo, research, partner, review, docs, support, rollout, and measurement links.
- Identify target developer segments, end users, implementers, affected constituencies, and missing owners.
- Separate facts, claims, hypotheses, commitments, unknowns, and contradictions.
- Identify whether a requested artifact is masking an earlier evidence/design decision.
- Run a preliminary user-impact and stakeholder scan.

## Artifacts

- One-page intake and lifecycle diagnosis
- Evidence/source inventory
- Stakeholder/owner map
- Highest-risk assumptions and questions
- Recommended phase/module and first five actions

## Common failures

- Accepting the requested launch date as the lifecycle stage
- Starting with content before understanding the decision
- Treating an API shape as proof of a problem
- Excluding end users because the requester is a developer/PM

## Transition packet

To incubation or the diagnosed phase: problem statement, users/jobs, current workaround, why browser intervention may fit, available evidence, material unknowns/contradictions, affected constituencies, owner/decision, and research plan.
