# Phase 04 — Wide review and readiness

## Entry

The design is approaching stability and needs broad standards, implementer, developer/framework, documentation, and cross-functional scrutiny before experiment or shipment.

## Decisions

- Is the proposal mature, independently implementable, testable, and suitable for the web?
- What do other engines, TAG/standards groups, developers/frameworks, affected users, and review disciplines actually say?
- Which objections require design/evidence changes?
- Is an experiment needed, and what uncertainty would it resolve?

## Work and evidence

- Request and track current TAG/vendor/standards positions with dates; never convert silence to support.
- Run source-grounded stakeholder critique and user-impact/formal-review rehearsal.
- Complete spec maturity, test/WPT strategy, interoperability/compatibility and Baseline path.
- Gather attributable Web/Framework Developer evidence with methods and limitations.
- Coordinate docs, samples, enterprise/admin, support, accessibility, privacy, security, performance, and debuggability preparation.
- Red-team likely public/press/regulatory questions and correct evidence presentation.

## Artifacts

- Wide-review matrix and response log
- Developer/framework evidence dossier
- Spec/test/interoperability state
- Pre-review user-impact matrix
- Documentation/sample/support plan
- Experiment-vs-ship decision brief

## Common failures

- Review requests sent too late to influence design
- Negative positions hidden in launch materials
- “No signal” treated as neutral/positive
- DevRel rehearsal described as formal approval
- General survey data used for proposal-specific demand

## Transition packet: wide review → experiment or prepare-to-ship

All positions/reviews and exact status; responses/design changes; unresolved objections and accepted risks; developer/partner evidence; spec/test/docs readiness; user cost/control matrix; interoperability/Baseline path; rationale for experiment or ship; learning/rollout conditions; and human approvals still required.
