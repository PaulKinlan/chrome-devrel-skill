# Phase 07 — Release communication

## Entry

The feature is entering beta/stable/default availability or a public trial milestone and requires accurate coordinated communication and support.

## Decisions

- Which audiences need what outcome, timing, and level of detail?
- What can be claimed now, and what remains experimental/limited?
- How will feedback, corrections, incidents, and rollout changes be handled?
- How does this feature connect to a wider developer/platform narrative?

## Work and evidence

- Publish/update one versioned source of truth for support, availability, costs, limitations, fallback, known issues, feedback, and changes.
- Coordinate reference docs, release notes, article/video, demos, deck, speaker notes, social, partner/GDE/event materials, support and localization/accessibility.
- Ensure claims match actual rollout by platform/channel/configuration.
- Monitor console/runtime failures, docs friction, support, standards/engine positions, partner use, press/public criticism, and abuse/team-safety signals.
- Correct quickly and publicly; pause/narrow/rollback when product evidence requires it.

## Artifacts

- Audience/channel launch plan
- Canonical narrative and evidence/claim sheet
- Published docs/demo/FAQ and DevRel-in-a-box pack
- Social/press/Q&A and correction/hold materials
- Live support/status/monitoring dashboard

## Common failures

- Announcement outruns implementation or commitment
- One generic message for developers, users, enterprises, and press
- Social hype omits limitations
- Technical criticism and harassment conflated
- Success measured only as impressions

## Transition packet: release → adoption/support

What shipped where; artifact/version index; launch reach and task outcomes; partner/framework uptake; support/friction themes; public/press criticism and factual corrections; incidents; open product/docs risks; adoption bottlenecks; monitoring cadence and owners.
