# Phase 03 — Developer trials

## Entry

A reasonably complete feature exists behind a flag/experimental build and representative developers can test it without deploying broadly to end users.

## Decisions

- Does the design solve realistic developer/user tasks?
- Which ergonomics, integration, safety, compatibility, or documentation problems appear in use?
- What should change before wider review or an Origin Trial?
- Is the problem/solution still supported by evidence?

## Work and evidence

- Recruit a deliberately varied cohort across segments, frameworks, device classes, geographies, accessibility needs, and deployment models.
- Provide task-based onboarding, samples, known limitations, fallback, issue/support routes, and consent for attributable feedback.
- Run observed friction logs/interviews, structured surveys, integration spikes, and office hours.
- Track who tried what, not just registrations; distinguish interest, evaluation, successful task, trial commitment, and production intent.
- Publish feedback themes/methods/limitations and contradictory evidence.
- Update product/design/docs/tests and re-run failed paths.

## Artifacts

- Cohort/recruitment and representativeness report
- Trial kit and feedback instruments
- Findings by task/segment/environment
- Updated partner evidence ladder
- Issue/design decision log and before/after friction evidence
- Wide-review/experiment recommendation

## Common failures

- Self-selected enthusiasts represented as all developers
- Vanity participation counts with no task completion
- Leading surveys or hidden sample sizes
- Trial support handled in private channels with no reusable learning

## Transition packet: developer trials → wide review or experiment

Cohort and methodology; task completion/friction; design changes; unresolved/contradictory feedback; attributable signals; integration/fallback evidence; user/review risks; implementation/docs/test state; explicit uncertainty requiring (or not requiring) field experiment; partner commitments; and next-stage decision criteria.
