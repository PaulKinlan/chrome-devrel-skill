# Community conduct

This project welcomes rigorous criticism of Chrome, web-platform proposals, DevRel practice, and the skill itself. Criticize ideas, evidence, decisions, and implementations—not people.

## Expected

- Be specific, evidence-based, and respectful.
- Disclose uncertainty and conflicts of interest where material.
- Steelman positions before rebutting them.
- Protect private, personal, and sensitive information.
- Give affected people room to disengage.

## Not acceptable

- Harassment, hate, threats, intimidation, or targeted abuse
- Doxxing or publishing private/personal information
- Impersonation or deliberate misrepresentation
- Spam, coordinated disruption, or repeatedly derailing technical discussion
- Pressuring an individual contributor to answer for an organization

Substantive disagreement is not a conduct violation. Moderation must not be used to hide inconvenient technical, ethical, regulatory, accessibility, privacy, security, or competition criticism.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may minimize, remove, or lock disruptive content and report serious violations to GitHub. Technical feedback should remain available through structured, moderated issues where possible.

Use GitHub's built-in **Report content** / **Report abuse** tools for harassment, threats, doxxing, or other policy violations. Do not repost sensitive content in a new issue. If there is an immediate safety risk, use appropriate local emergency and organizational security channels rather than this repository.
